

# Project 1: Decoding Credit Risk
## A Data-Driven Lending Analysis
**Analyst:** Josephine Hart
**Date:** April 2026

--------------------------------------------

## Project Overview

This project analyzes 1,000 loan applicants to uncover credit risk patterns across demographics, housing types, and loan purposes — translating complex financial data into actionable lending insights.

## Business Question

*What factors most influence loan risk, and what should lending managers prioritize when assessing loan applicants?*

## Tools Used
- *Excel* — data cleaning, pivot analysis, and  dashboard
- *SQL* — querying and aggregating loan data across multiple dimensions
- *Power BI* — interactive dashboard and visual storytelling

## Key Findings

-  *Total credit portfolio* analyzed: *3 million* across 1,000 applicants
-  *Average credit amount: 3.27K | **Average loan duration*: 20.90 months
-  *Housing type matters* — applicants in free housing carried the highest average credit amounts, followed by rent and own
-  *Vacation/other purposes* attracted the highest average credit amounts, while radio/TV loans were the lowest
-  *69% of applicants were male*, 31% female
-  *Top 10 highest risk combinations* identified by housing type and loan purpose, with vacation/other + free housing emerging as the riskiest profile
- *Car loans* had the highest total credit volume by purpose

## Recommendations

-  *Tighten screening* for applicants in free housing seeking vacation/other loans — this combination consistently appeared in the highest risk profiles
-  *Request additional documentation* from applicants with no savings account and little checking account balance before approving high credit amounts
-  *Cap loan durations* for high-risk purpose categories to reduce long-term exposure
-  *Review gender lending balance* — with 69% male applicants, ensure credit decisions are based purely on financial indicators and not demographic bias
-  *Monitor car loan portfolio closely* given its high total credit volume, as it represents significant concentration risk
-  *Use the Top 10 risk combinations dashboard* as a real-time screening tool during loan approval processes
