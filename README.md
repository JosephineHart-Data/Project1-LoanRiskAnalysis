


# Project 1: Decoding Credit Risk
## A Data-Driven Lending Analysis
**Analyst:** Josephine Hart
**Date:** April 2026
**Tools:** PostgreSQL | Excel | Power BI

---

## Business Question
What factors most influence loan risk and what 
should lending officers prioritize when 
assessing applicants?

---

## Dataset
- Source: UCI German Credit Dataset via Kaggle
- Size: 1,000 records, 10 variables

---

## Key Insights
1. Vacation/other loans carry the highest average
   credit amount (8,209) despite being the least 
   common loan type
2. Free housing applicants borrow the most on 
   average (4,906) despite having no housing costs
3. Car loans represent the highest total financial 
   exposure at 1,269,881
4. 69% of applicants are male

---

## Business Recommendations
1. Apply strictest screening to vacation/other 
   loans — highest individual risk per borrower
2. Flag free housing applicants for additional 
   financial accountability checks
3. Prioritize car loan portfolio monitoring as it 
   represents the largest total exposure
4. Consider gender-inclusive lending policies to 
   attract more female applicants
